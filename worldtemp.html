Global Measles Outbreaks

WHO Data from 2011 - 2019

This interactive chart allows you to visualize the global burden of measles between 2011 and 2019. You’ll notice that while the number of cases in the US has increased in recent years, other countries have experienced a variety of trends. You can sort this chart by country, increase in cases, total number of cases and continent, or search for an individual country that you’re interested in.

Background information

Even with implementation of routine immunization, measles continues to circulate globally due to suboptimal vaccination coverage and population immunity gaps. Any community with less than 95% population immunity is at risk for an outbreak. If an outbreak response is not timely and comprehensive, the virus will find its way into more pockets of vulnerable individuals and potentially spread within and beyond the affected countries.

As long as measles continues to circulate anywhere in the world, no country can be assured to avoid importation. The WHO recommends that countries protect their populations through high vaccine coverage achieved primarily through routine immunization programs, and where necessary through supplemental immunization activities designed to assure that susceptible individuals are vaccinated.
