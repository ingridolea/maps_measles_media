Measles Outbreaks in the United States

Week-by-week Data in 2019

Measles was once thought to be eradicated in the United States. After decades through America’s early history, national vigilance around vaccination supported the country’s herd immunity to such a degree that the disease seemed like part of the country’s past rather than present. Throughout recent decades, however, measles has become increasingly difficult to control, with outbreaks becoming more commonplace amidst faltering vaccination rates.

Media insight: What's with NYC?

The 2019 New York measles outbreak was a series of measles cases immersed in considerable dialogue around public health, religious freedom, and vaccine hesitancy. The combination of a high concentration of people who chose to remain unvaccinated for religious reasons and the population density of New York City sparked heated controversy regarding the ethics of vaccine exemptions. In April of 2019 New York City mayor Bill DeBlasio declared a public health emergency, requiring everyone over six months old in the neighborhoods of Williamsburg and Borough Park to be vaccinated. The New York City Health Department finally declared the outbreak to b over in September of 2019.
