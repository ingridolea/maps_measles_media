Measles are an even bigger problem internationally

The disease burden of measles by country and continent in 2019

In 2019, the incidence of measles, i.e. the number of new cases recorded, was high. There were a total of 429,650 reported cases of measles worldwide. Sizeable outbreaks were recorded across different regions of the world, inter alia in the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Ethiopia, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Madagascar, Myanmar, Philippines, Sudan, Thailand and Ukraine.

Measles incidence - The majority of measles cases in 2019 were recorded in Africa, especially Madagascar

This visualization illustrates the global distribution of measles cases in 2019. Country blocks are organized by continent and each country includes the total number of cases reported in that country from January 1 - December 31, 2019. Based on data from the WHO, get the data here. Visualization adapted from D3 Source Treemap by Mike Bostock

When considering the number of measles cases per country, it is important to consider the population of that country to get an accurate pictue of the burden the disease is imposing on a country. The corresponding epidemiological metric - disease prevalence - captures the proportion of a particular population found to be affected by a disease.

Measles prevalence - African and European countries (in roughly equal measure) have the highest number of measles cases relative to their population
