Maps, Measles, and Media

A Code 4 Policy 2020 Project

What is measles?

Measles is a highly contagious viral disease. It remains an important cause of death among young children globally, despite the availability of a safe and effective vaccine.

Learn More

Why maps?

Once declared transmission free, measles has made a spectacular comeback in the US. In 2019, there were 1,282 individual cases of measles confirmed in 31 states. The ability to visualize these outbreaks, both in the US and globally, may inspire action.

Learn More

What about the media?

The recent outbreaks in the US and other countries have sparked great media interest and public debate about measles. This website seeks to provide analysis and background information to enable readers to make sense of they are reading and hearing about measles.

Learn More
